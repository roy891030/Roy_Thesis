    \textbf{Abstract} \\[0.5cm]

\normalsize

This study examines whether graph-attention-based modeling improves stock selection and return prediction in the Taiwan stock market. Using daily TEJ data, we build 56 technical features, an industry graph, and a market-wide graph, and compare five models: Linear Regression, XGBoost, LSTM, GATRegressor, and DMFM within a reproducible pipeline for data processing, training, and evaluation. Performance is evaluated by RMSE, MAE, IC, ICIR, and directional accuracy, while portfolio validation ranks stocks at each rebalancing date, takes equal-weight long positions in the top names, and compares the results with the 0050 benchmark.

Results show that model superiority depends on both the horizon and the evaluation layer. In the Short window, XGBoost delivers the highest IC (0.0863) and annualized return (100.2231\%); in the Medium window, DMFM attains the highest IC (0.0780) and annualized return (39.0107\%). In the Long window, DMFM raises ICIR from 0.3322 under Linear Regression to 0.8905, while also achieving the best annualized return, Sharpe ratio, and win rate at 44.4274\%, 1.5261, and 70.6897\%, respectively. Relative to the 0050 benchmark, DMFM improves the Long-window annualized return from 19.3974\% to 44.4274\%, the Sharpe ratio from 0.6253 to 1.5261, and the win rate from 58.6207\% to 70.6897\%. Overall, the combination of graph attention and multi-factor representation improves the stability and practical usability of stock-selection signals, especially over medium- to long-horizon settings.
