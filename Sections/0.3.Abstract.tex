    \textbf{Abstract} \\[0.5cm]

\end{center}

\normalsize

This study examines how model architecture, graph topology, and training-window length affect cross-sectional return ranking and portfolio performance in the Taiwan equity market. Rather than claiming that GAT is uniformly superior, the thesis frames graph attention as an ablation setting for relation modeling and factor representation, evaluated jointly by IC / ICIR and portfolio-level risk-adjusted performance.

Using daily TEJ data, we construct 56 technical and valuation features and define the prediction target as the cross-sectionally demeaned five-day forward return. The main experiment uses an industry graph based on same-industry complete connections and a universe graph based on market-wide complete connections. The model set includes Linear, XGBoost, LSTM, DNN, GAT-industry, GAT-universe, GAT-TwoGraph, GAT-IndNeutral, and GAT-full. Three training windows are tested under chronological train-validation-test splits for short, medium, and long settings. Portfolio validation ranks stocks every five trading days, takes equal-weight long positions in the top 10\%, and compares the strategy with the 0050 benchmark.

The results show that training-window length is the dominant driver of out-of-sample usability. Short-window models can produce positive Daily IC, but most portfolios have near-zero or negative annualized returns. Medium and long windows are required for stable positive Sharpe ratios. In the long window, the GAT neutralization family achieves an average Daily IC of 0.0587 and an average Sharpe ratio of 1.826, compared with 0.0502 and 1.704 for the GAT family and 0.0535 and 1.564 for DNN. Graph topology also matters: GAT-industry outperforms GAT-universe in both Daily IC and Sharpe under medium and long windows, suggesting that economically meaningful industry links preserve stock-level ranking information better than a naive all-to-all universe graph. GAT-IndNeutral obtains the highest long-window Daily IC (0.0590) and ICIR (0.786), while GAT-full achieves the highest Sharpe ratio (1.849) and 42.02\% annualized return, revealing a trade-off between pure alpha stability and absolute portfolio return. XGBoost reaches the highest long-window total return (143.08\%), but its train IC of 0.7824 and test IC of 0.0205 indicate severe overfitting risk. Overall, the contribution of graph-based models should be interpreted as gains in stability, topology selection, and interpretability rather than a uniform dominance in absolute performance.
